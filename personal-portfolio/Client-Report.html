Client Report

How did you decide what content and images to use for your site plan?

There weren't many images for the website in concept so I didn't really include those. The content of the website was supposed to be representative of user submitted content so I just gave the developer some random examples of things that I had written previously as it would be fairly representative of what you might see on that sort of website.

How did you communicate with your developer? Was there any issues with communication?

Me and the developer communicated in person and through a online chat app. Primarily we communicated in person during class as there wasn't a great deal to discuss. So in person communications and discussion sufficed for the most part.

Did your developer meet your expectations for your site?

I would say so. A full excution of the website concept would be beyond the scope of this class. But given the scope of the class it was a fair representation of what the website could end up as.

What will you do differently next time you hire a team to do a project for you?

I will aim to have a more robust goal and vision for the project.

General Experince and Reflections

What was the most challenging part of this project for you? Were you able to overcome it?

The hardest part of this project for me was the CSS. It is a quibbly sort of thing to use and to be frank I don't know how much I really care for it. But I chipped away at it bit by bit, working on each element until it was roughly where I wanted it.

What did you enjoy most about this project?

I think I might have most enjoyed being able to push my developer out of their comfort zone. Not to say that I'm certain that I did. But I ended up making them work in an unfamiliar visual style that I don't believe they had any previous experience with or knowledge of.

What would you do differently if you could do the project again?

I would make the project design I gave to my developer more difficult to execute. Generally a more complex design with some more complex elements. It would be interesting to see how far they could push their skills.

Do you feel more confident in working on real-world projects after this experience?

Somewhat. The project was a new experience for me in that in all my previous projects I have been working to meet my own design and expectations rather than someone else's. So it was something that I hadn't previously experinced to work on realizing a design that I had not part in the creation of. Considering that this is closer to the usual experince in the workplace I think this initial exposure is a good foundation moving forward.
